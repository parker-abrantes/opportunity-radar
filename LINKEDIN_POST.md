# LinkedIn Post Draft

I built a local-first Opportunity Radar to make internship, research, scholarship, and conference travel-grant searching feel less chaotic.

The idea is simple: instead of keeping dozens of tabs open and manually deciding what matters, I wanted one ranked queue that can separate strong-fit opportunities from noise.

The dashboard:

- scores opportunities by role alignment, mission fit, eligibility, funding, urgency, and effort-to-value
- filters for internships, research roles, systems/TPM work, nuclear/energy opportunities, local roles, and travel grants
- connects strong internship/research fits into an application tracker
- lets me pass on expired or weak-fit opportunities without losing the audit trail
- runs locally on my laptop, using CSV files instead of a heavy backend

I started building this because I care about opportunities across nuclear energy, aerospace, autonomy, data-center energy, and technical program management, but I do not want the search process itself to become the main project.

This is still a prototype, but it is already changing how I think about career systems: the goal is not to apply to everything. The goal is to build a calm, repeatable process for noticing the right things early and acting on them intentionally.
